Intuitive priors generator

Turn an intuitive guess — a single estimate plus a plausible range — into Beta(α, β) parameters you can drop straight into a Bayesian analysis.

Use this tool when:

  • You are studying a proportion, such as task completion rates or survey agreement.
  • You have an intuitive sense of the likely value: a best guess and a range of plausible outcomes.
  • For example, you might guess 80% task completion, and would be surprised below 70% or above 90%.

Why use a prior:

  • Ruling out unreasonable values sharpens inference — you may report 95% directional confidence instead of 90%.
  • A prior is mathematically equivalent to extra data, effectively increasing your sample size for free.
  • Uncomfortable with strong priors? Provide a very wide range to get a weak, nearly-flat prior.
